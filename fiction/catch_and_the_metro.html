Catch and the metro

It was at my grandma's where my story started to unfold. I realized that I was alone. Too alone. Not another living soul, anywhere. Just some luggage discarded in the backyard. And stuff which did not have any right to be in the backyard. Greyish stuff. Not entirely opaque. Better not looking up, I knew that the source of the light here was not a bright autumnal sun.

Some of the stuff belonged to my past, the one I experienced a couple of years ago. More belonged to a more distant past, the one I could not remember, or, at the very least, I ought not to remember. And the rest were from another universe, from where my grandma might buy it once, or might have bought it already if the time had taken a different path.

In any case, it wasn't my grandma's backyard I was used to. Not a universe I was used to. I had to test whether the laws of physics were different from the ones I knew.

Inside, one door didn't have a handle. Pure provocation. Even though I wasn't able to slide through them like a ghost, I managed to open them somehow. And close them again, after I found behind them the usual, normal, bedroom as it always had been.

Heading back out across a glass patio, I noticed the affairs in the backyard returned to their more usual state. Quacking, clucking, and barking and in the middle of the commotion, my grandma. She hadn't noticed me though. I tried to get out, but an aggressive duck flew into me, a mother and a stepmother of a large flock of ducklings peacefully grazing all around the house. I was forced to retreat. Or was it two ducks? Yeah, definitely two mean ducks.

Since the front door was not a viable option, I had to find a different way out. Sideway. I just stepped aside to think on it, not realizing there was supposed to be a wall of glass and bricks, barely aware of that fact. I was out.

Grandma's patio ends with a small staircase, so I ended quite a bit up above the ground. It was a time to experiment with flying...

Leaving the backyard behind, I went on to explore the tree tops behind a meadow. My eyes got caught by a beautiful arrangement of a young tree with colorful leaves, dry grass and huge green burdock leaves. I looked at it from all the sides and regretted that I forgot my camera. I told myself I would have to return later.

After buzzing around the meadow, I returned to my beautiful arrangement. What a surprise, it was no longer there. It was just a normal piece of a meadow just like the rest. Time to move on, towards the street.

Flying above the street, I haven't noticed I was losing altitude and that the houses along started to lose their shape, being replaced by something else entirely. I was startled to realize I was suddenly walking on a paved path. The greyish blue setts were layed out in a pattern that caught my attention, mesmerizing me to forget the surprise. As the road collected tributaries and sank into the ground, the walls were tiled in the same pattern.

Taking in the majestic architecture of an anonymous city that I was leaving behind, I walked the length of the subterranean corridor and ended up at a crossroads. Stairway on the right, stairway on the left. After a short hesitation, I decided to go left. I knew where the stairs would lead. To the metro.

The metro station was even more interesting than the maze of corridors. Entering, I could see the whole length of it. Orange brown tiles, but in the same mesmerizing pattern as before. To my right and behind, the rail tracks led into a dark tunnel. My end of the station was flooded with artificial light, the front of the station was bathing in daylight. However, the two sources of light somehow managed to harmonize into a tasteful play of glows and shadows.

Contrary to the empty corridors, the station was full of people. Well, half full. A usual mix of anonymous folk, though slightly more cartoonish, less real than the other details. The details were all around, wherever my eyes rested. Lamps were full of history, nooks and crannies popped out from the background, smudges on the floor revealed stories. The billboards were detailed all over too, except where the ads would have been. Perhaps I just didn't want to see any ads.

The rail tracks were almost at the same level as the platform, just a step lower. And in between the tracks for both directions, there was a third pair of rails. A very unusual one, with a variable gauge. I could see with my own eyes how an entering train switched the the middle track. The wheels came closer together and further apart smoothly, without forcing the train to change speed. A slow speed, but nevertheless.

The purpose of the variable railway gauge remained unknown to me, the train returned to its original track and left into the tunnel. Whether it stopped or not to let people exit or enter, I couldn't remember. Maybe they did so while it was moving. In any case, people were crossing the tracks, to get to the other platform, all around the train. Not like any metro I knew, more like a tram, but not quite. I crossed to the other side too and not knowing what to do, I waited for my train.

I had been interrupted from my waiting by a young man who threw a ball in my direction. An unusual ball that had an inner glow. Not monochromatic and not multiple colors at once. More like a shadow play on a single hue, while the hue changed secretly whenever I looked away.

He'd always thrown it towards me, it would touch the ground, bounce in the original direction and then, unexpectedly, return to its owner. Like a boomerang that could bounce from surfaces. The ball radiated an impression of confidence, as if bouncing into impossible directions was a skill it learned when it was young and now it achieved an aura of elegance about it.

What the heck, I asked myself. I didn't feel like playing catch, especially not with such a strange ball. Nevertheless, I couldn't avert my eyes from it. Thinking about it, thinking about the direction it moved, thinking about the direction I would like it to move, it started to listen to my thoughts as well.

Thus I started to play a game of catch. Sometimes throwing the ball with mostly my hands, other times reaching for it purely with my thoughts. Once, I managed to lose it to the tunnel, which was downhill by the way. Maybe that would have been the end and I could have happily returned to my train waiting.

The tall young man had other plans however, he saved the ball with a bout of willpower. The friend who joined him now might have had to help him or maybe not, they were now two to one. Luckily, a fellow traveller joined my side, turning the game into doubles.

As I started to enjoyed myself, the world started to blur on the edges, getting translucent and dark. I woke up for one last time to tell you my story. The story that would begin with a train arrival.

The train that would take me from this darkness to a world of daylight.
